A good approach is to maintain **two design systems** that use the same semantic meaning but different visual intensity:

* **Dark Theme:** Bright, neon colors that stand out on dark backgrounds.
* **Light Theme:** Soft, pastel colors that are easy on the eyes and don't overpower the UI.

### Dark Theme (Neon)

These colors work best on backgrounds like `#111827`, `#0F172A`, or `#1E1E2E`, providing high contrast and quick recognition in a busy kitchen.

### Light Theme (Pastel)

These pair well with backgrounds like `#FFFFFF`, `#FAFAFA`, or `#F8FAFC`, offering a softer appearance while preserving the same semantic meaning as the dark theme.

This paired system ensures users instantly recognize statuses across themes without needing to relearn the color meanings.
